Welcome to the
Japan Society of Northern Ireland

The Japan Society of Northern Ireland is an entirely voluntary organisation established on 17th September 1996 to promote a greater understanding within Northern Ireland of Japan and its people, culture, and language, and to foster greater communication between both countries.

JSNI has organised and promoted a variety of cultural, educational, and social events. These have included the first ever Nihon Buyo performance in Northern Ireland, a Koto concert, Japanese tea ceremony, Ikebana and calligraphy workshops, Kimono and Japanese cookery demonstrations, and seminars on doing business with Japan. Other events include family picnics, Christmas parties, New Year dinners, and pub nights.
